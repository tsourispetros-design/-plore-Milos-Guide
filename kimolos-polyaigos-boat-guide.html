FULL DAY ROUTE Suggested Route

ROUTE OVERVIEW

Depart from Agios Sostis, Milos. Head northwest across the channel to Kimolos — three beach stops. Continue southwest to Polyaigos for three more stops. Return to Agios Sostis before sunset.

Total distance: approximately 45 nautical miles. Allow 8–9 hours including stops. Depart early — no later than 9:00 AM.

Depart by 9:00 AM at the latest
Monitor wind direction throughout the day
If conditions worsen at Kimolos, return to Milos — do not continue to Polyaigos

KIMOLOS — FIRST STOP
SWIM STOP · KIMOLOS Prassa

KIMOLOS STOP 01

The most famous beach on Kimolos. White volcanic sand, marble rock formations and stunning turquoise water. Anchor offshore and swim in — the bay is protected from most wind directions.

Anchor in sandy bottom — check holding before swimming
Thermal waters near the pier on the south side
Can be busy — watch for other boats and swimmers

BOAT ACCESS ONLY · KIMOLOS Soufi

KIMOLOS STOP 02

A completely secluded beach on the southwest coast of Kimolos — no road access. Soft golden sand and shallow turquoise water. One of the most pristine spots in the Cyclades.

Usually empty — enjoy the solitude
Shallow entry — ideal for swimming and snorkeling
Exposed to southwest winds — skip if wind is from SW

SCENIC SNORKEL · KIMOLOS Mavrospilia

KIMOLOS STOP 03

Rocky islets, crystal water and a dramatic sunset backdrop. Excellent snorkeling around the volcanic rock formations. The area also contains the ruins of an ancient sunken city underwater.

Best snorkeling spot on the route — explore the rocky seabed
Ancient ruins visible underwater near the shore
Rocky seabed — anchor carefully and check the anchor

POLYAIGOS — SECOND STOP
BUSY · POLYAIGOS Πάνω Μυρσίνη

POLYAIGOS STOP 01

A stunning wild beach on the uninhabited island of Polyaigos. Expect many boats, catamarans and yachts — especially in July and August. Move slowly, anchor well away from others.

Very busy in peak season — large catamarans and yachts present
Move at minimum speed — no-wake zone near the beach
Anchor at least 30 meters from other boats
Check anchor every time before swimming

CROWDED · POLYAIGOS Παραλία Πολύαιγος

POLYAIGOS STOP 02

The main anchorage of Polyaigos — turquoise water, white cliffs and an untouched wild landscape. One of the most beautiful bays in the Aegean, but often packed with vessels from all over Greece.

Dozens of boats may be anchored — patience required
Watch for swimmer zones — boats entering and exiting constantly
Stay minimum 50 meters from the shoreline
Do not leave the boat unattended without checking the anchor

BLUE WATERS · POLYAIGOS Γαλάζια Νερά

POLYAIGOS STOP 03

Named for its impossibly vivid blue water, this cove on the southern side of Polyaigos is quieter than the main beach. Approach slowly and check depth before anchoring.

Calmer and less crowded than the main bay
Excellent snorkeling in the clear shallow water
Rocky approach — reduce speed well before entering
Check wind direction — exposed to south and west

SAFETY ESSENTIALS

Open water rules

This route crosses open sea between islands. These rules are essential — not optional.

Check wind forecast every morning before departure. Do not sail above Beaufort 4.
If weather changes mid-route, anchor safely and contact us on WhatsApp immediately.
Polyaigos is uninhabited — there are no services, no help and no shade on land.
Always check the anchor before anyone enters the water.
At Polyaigos, move slowly at all times — many boats, swimmers and catamarans.
Never force the boat into tight spaces between other anchored vessels.
Stay at least 100 meters from rocks and shallow reefs.
Keep life jackets accessible at all times on this route.
